01 / Monash College

1. Passport to Maths (Monash College)

Project Overview

At Monash College, I contributed to the award-recognised Passport to Maths program, which received Runner-Up for the ACODE Award for Excellence at the ASCILITE Conference. It was a self-paced Moodle learning experience designed to support students entering maths units with varied levels of prior knowledge. The project focused on improving diagnostic clarity, student engagement and accessibility through structured modules, visual explanations, interactive questions and clearer feedback.

My Role & Contribution

My role focused on improving the way maths content was presented online through video, visual design, Moodle content structure and activity support.

I helped create and refine lightboard video resources, edited learning videos, developed supporting visuals and infographics, and assisted with turning complex maths explanations into clearer digital learning materials.

Negotiating and remodeling our content as per stakeholder feedback requested changes and modifications was also a big part of the job.

Constraints

The project needed to make technical maths content more accessible, engaging and easier to follow in an online self-paced format. A key challenge was balancing accurate maths content, complex STACK question logic, video production quality, accessibility requirements and a tight project timeline. Tight timeline; complex STACK logic; consistent accessibility across media.

Goals & Metrics

Mathematics content had to be technically accurate, visually clear and easy for students to follow in Moodle. A text-heavy approach would not have supported the learner experience well enough.

Increase diagnostic completion; reduce recurring error patterns; provide cleaner placement signal.

Design 7 Build Strategy (Pedagogy + UX)

I led the pedagogy and the end-to-end video production workflow so complex maths explanations worked as both learning design and polished media. That included scripting, lightboard staging, audio capture, lighting setup, teleprompter support, and post-production editing to create clear, accessible Moodle videos.

Production details covered key and fill lighting, background control, clean audio with lavalier and directional mics, prompter operation for smooth delivery, and editing with captions, visual annotations, pacing and quality checks. The goal was to make the maths content feel trustworthy, easy to follow and usable in a self-paced digital learning environment.

Outcome / Value

The project helped transform maths preparation and diagnostic content into a more engaging and accessible online learning experience. Stronger diagnostic signal for placement.

My contribution helped improve the quality of video production and time management and consistency of the digital learning materials.

Artifacts

  • Passport to Maths product explainer - product explainer video reference.

What I'd Improve Next

Adaptive branches by misconception category; A/B test hint timing and example density to improve student confidence and completion.

02 / Victoria University

2. Block Model – Digital Learning Redesign and LMS Support (Victoria University)

Project Overview

At Victoria University, I supported the design, development and improvement of online learning spaces for units delivered through the VU Block Model. The work focused on helping teaching teams structure learning content, activities and assessments clearly within D2L Brightspace, while supporting consistent online delivery, student engagement and staff confidence with digital learning tools.

My Role & Contribution

I worked as a Learning Design Support Officer, collaborating with learning designers, educators, subject matter experts and project teams. My role sat across learning design support, LMS setup, content organisation, staff guidance, quality checking and digital learning troubleshooting.

I assisted with building and refining Brightspace unit spaces, organising learning materials, checking activity and assessment flow, and helping staff use tools such as H5P, Panopto, Turnitin, Zoom and other digital learning technologies. I also created help guides, supported online professional development sessions, responded to LMS and learning technology queries, and helped translate technical processes into clear instructions for teaching staff.

The Challenge

The Block Model required online units to be clear, consistent and easy for students to follow in a shorter, more focused teaching format. Staff needed support with LMS structure, assessment setup, digital tools and practical troubleshooting. A key challenge was balancing learning design quality, accessibility, staff capability, technology support and tight delivery timelines across multiple units.

Design & Build Approach

The approach focused on creating clearer unit structures, improving the flow between learning materials, activities and assessments, and supporting staff to use digital tools in ways that improved teaching and learning. I helped organise content into logical sections, review pages for consistency, support H5P and multimedia use, and ensure resources were easier for students to access and navigate.

I also contributed to staff-facing support by preparing step-by-step guides, assisting with workshops and helping resolve common LMS issues. This work combined practical learning design, digital learning support, user experience thinking and LMS administration.

Outcome / Value

The project supported smoother online and blended delivery within the VU Block Model. It helped teaching staff improve the structure and usability of digital learning spaces, supported more consistent LMS delivery, and gave staff clearer guidance when using learning technologies. My contribution strengthened the connection between learning design, LMS support and practical staff capability building.

03 / Padua College

3. Subject Selection Site Redesign — Padua College

Project Overview

At Padua College, I contributed to improving the online subject selection experience for students, families and staff. The work focused on making the subject selection space clearer, easier to navigate and more useful during the intake and course selection process. The project involved improving Moodle page structure, content hierarchy, navigation, buttons, cards and support information so users could better understand where to go and what action to take.

The work supported the subject selection workflow across multiple intake cycles and connected with systems and processes such as Moodle, Select My Subjects and SchoolData.

My Role & Contribution

My role focused on improving the digital learning and information experience inside Moodle. I helped redesign the subject selection area by reviewing the existing structure, identifying confusing or unclear sections, and creating a cleaner layout with clearer navigation, page sections and support points.

I created different visual layout options, improved page organisation, added clearer headings, cards and buttons, and helped make the space easier for students, families and staff to use. I also contributed to refining the content based on feedback and practical use during the subject selection cycle.

Constraints

The site needed to support different users at the same time: students making choices, families trying to understand information, and staff managing the process. The challenge was to make the information clear without overloading the page.

The work also needed to fit within existing school systems, Moodle structure, school branding, and timelines connected to the subject selection process.

Goals & Success Metrics

The main goal was to reduce confusion and make the subject selection process easier to follow.

Success could be measured through clearer navigation, fewer repeated questions, easier access to key information, smoother staff support, and a more consistent structure for future subject selection cycles.

Design & Build Strategy

The design approach focused on information architecture, visual hierarchy and user flow.

I organised content into clearer sections, used headings and visual groupings to reduce cognitive load, and created a more structured Moodle page experience. Cards, buttons and support prompts were used to guide users toward the right information and actions.

The layout was designed to feel more like a guided digital experience rather than a long information page.

Outcome / Value

The redesigned subject selection space improved clarity, navigation and usability for students, families and staff. It created a more organised digital hub for subject selection information and gave the school a clearer structure that could be reused or refined for later intake cycles.

My contribution helped connect learning technology, UX thinking and school process improvement in a practical Moodle-based project.

  • Subject selection workflow diagram

What I'd Improve Next

I would add clearer analytics or feedback collection to understand which sections students and families use most. I would also improve the page with more guided pathways for different user groups, such as students, parents and staff.

04 / Padua College

4. Year 7 Social Media Awareness Module — Padua College

Project Overview

At Padua College, I developed a Year 7 Social Media Awareness module in Moodle. The module was designed to support students in learning about online behaviour, safety, awareness and responsible digital participation through a structured online learning experience.

The project included five structured learning sections, interactive activities and a completion certificate. It was built to give students a clear pathway through the content while allowing staff to track progress and support completion.

My Role & Contribution

My role focused on structuring, building and refining the Moodle learning experience. I worked with the relevant staff and subject matter input to clarify the purpose of the module, organise the content into a logical sequence, build activities, test the student flow and check completion settings.

I helped turn the topic into a more engaging Moodle-based learning module rather than a static information page. I also supported the practical setup of activities and completion requirements so the experience could be delivered consistently to students.

Constraints

The module needed to be age-appropriate for Year 7 students, easy to follow, and structured enough for staff to monitor. The content needed to be clear, simple and practical without becoming too text-heavy.

A key challenge was balancing student-friendly language, interactive learning, Moodle functionality, completion tracking and certificate settings within the available timeline.

Goals & Success Metrics

The goal was to create a structured and accessible Moodle module that students could complete independently or with teacher guidance.

Success could be measured through student completion, staff sign-off, clear module progression, reduced confusion, and successful certificate generation after completion.

Design & Build Strategy

The design approach focused on structured learning, interaction and completion flow.

The content was divided into five clear sections, with activities placed in a sequence that supported understanding and progression. Moodle completion settings were used to guide students through the module and support tracking.

Interactive activities helped move the module away from passive reading and toward a more active learning experience. The design also considered clarity, readability and practical use in a school environment.

Outcome / Value

The module provided a complete digital learning experience for Year 7 students and supported the school’s social media awareness education. It gave students a clearer structure for learning and gave staff a Moodle-based system for managing delivery and completion.

My contribution helped turn the content into a more usable, interactive and trackable learning module.

Artifacts

  • Module map or section overview
  • Screenshots of the five Moodle sections
  • Interactive activity examples
  • Completion settings screenshot
  • Certificate example
  • Student flow diagram

What I'd Improve Next

I would add more scenario-based branching activities, short reflection questions and simple student feedback data to understand where students engage most or need more support.
